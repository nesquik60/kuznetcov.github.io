Types of banks

• The banking sector in the UK is made up of a variety of institutions supervised by the country’s central bank, The Bank of England, which looks after the government’s finance and monetary policy and acts as banker to other banks
• Clearing bank – in the UK, a bank that is a member of a clearing house (= an organisation that exchange payments between customers of different banks)
• Commercial bank – a bank with branches in many different places, that provides a range of services, especially related current accounts, loans and saving money, for people and businesses.
• Merchant bank – a bank that deals with large businesses, this bank is particularly active in in arranging mergers and acquisitions and in advising on aspects of corporate finance.
