Direct and indirect methods of selling

Direct selling is the practice of a manufacturer or importer in selling goods direct to shopkeepers without using the service of wholesaler or distributor.

Indirect selling is the practice of a manufacturer or importer in selling goods to wholesaler, then they sells goods to ”middleman”, after that the goods are sold to the retailer outlet and in the end the goods are sold to the customers.

The methods of selling are direct, when the retail outlets aren`t used.

Types:
a) Mail order – a company sends goods by post from its warehouse;
b) Door-to-door sales – an agent for the company sells the product or service to the customer at home;
c) TV sales – customers see product adverts on the screen and place their orders by phone/fax/the Internet;
d) The Internet (e-commerce) – electronic shopping from companies` websites;
e) Cash and carry – customers can buy from the manufacturer`s warehouse.
The customer often buys a product or service from a retail outlet (indirect way).
a) Supermarket – a large self-service shop selling food and drink and also small household items;
b) Hypermarket –a very large supermarket often locations on the edge of a town or city;
c) Shopping center/mall – a covered area with shops, supermarkets and restaurants;
d) Department store – a large shop with many departments or sections – each department sells a different type of goods;
e) Specialist retailer – shop which only sells one type of product, usually of high – quality;
f) Chain store – one of a group of shops owned by the same company.
