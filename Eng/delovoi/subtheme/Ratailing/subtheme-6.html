Types of retail outlets

• supermarket – a large self-service shop selling food and drink and also small household items.
• hypermarket - a very large supermarket often locations on the edge of a town or city.
• department store - a large shop with many departments or sections - each department sells a different type of goods.
• chain store - one of a group of shops owned by the same company.
• Shopping center/mall - a covered area with shops, supermarkets and restaurants.
• Specialist retailer - shop which only sells one type of product, usually of high-quality.
• Mail order - a company sends goods by post from its warehouse.
• Door-to-door sales - an agent for the company sells the product or service to the customer at home.
• The Internet (e-commerce) - Electronic shopping from companies' websites.
• Cash and carry - a customer can buy from the manufacture's warehouse.
