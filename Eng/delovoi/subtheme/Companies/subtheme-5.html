Company’s structure

Most companies are made up of three groups of people: the shareholders, the management and the workforce. The management consists of 4 levels: Board of directors, then Managing director, then Senior management, and Middle management. At the top of the company of hierarchy is the Board of directors, headed by the Chalperson or President. The Board is responsible for policy decisions and strategy. It will usually appoint a Managing Director or Chief Executive Officer, who has overall responsibility for the running of business. Senior managers or company officers head the various departments on functions within company, which may include the following: marketing, Public relations, Information Technology or IT, Personal or Human recourses, Finance, Production, Research and Development or R and D
